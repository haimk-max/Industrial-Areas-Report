## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Zone Overview
**Raanana Industrial Zone** is classified as **MEDIUM RISK (בינוני)** for groundwater contamination based on the 2021 Water Quality Assessment Report. The zone contains chemical manufacturing, pharmaceutical production, and light manufacturing facilities distributed across approximately 1-2 km² in the northern coastal aquifer.

### Key Findings (1999-2008 Historical Analysis)
1. **TCE (Trichloroethylene)** shows **increasing trend** in shallow aquifer (borehole R-001) at +2.84 ppb/year, indicating ongoing or recent contamination source
2. **1,2-DCA (1,2-Dichloroethane)** detected at extreme levels (up to 100 ppb in 1999) with improvement trend but remains elevated
3. **Deep aquifer contamination** confirmed at 88.5m depth (borehole R-004), indicating historical contamination with slow natural attenuation
4. **Persistent chloroform** background contamination suggesting older spill events

### Risk Assessment
- **5 monitoring boreholes** operational in zone with varying risk classifications
- **Primary concern**: TCE in shallow aquifer (R-001) with upward trend
- **Secondary concern**: 1,2-DCA persistence at depth (R-004) despite improvement
- **Zone position**: Above-average contamination relative to other 17 zones in coastal aquifer system

### Recommendation
**CONTINUE INTENSIVE MONITORING** with particular focus on TCE trend reversal and 1,2-DCA attenuation rate. Recommend quarterly sampling for high-priority boreholes and source facility operational review.

## CONTAMINATION ANALYSIS

### Detected Contaminant Families

#### 1. CHLORINATED VOLATILE ORGANIC COMPOUNDS (VOCs)
**TCE (Trichloroethylene)**
- **Prevalence**: 4/5 boreholes
- **Trend**: INCREASING in R-001 (+2.84 ppb/year, R²=0.88)
- **Severity**: Highest (31.8 ppb peak in R-001, 2006)
- **Source**: Chemical synthesis operations (likely Facility I-001)
- **Concern**: Increasing trend suggests ongoing source or recent release

**1,2-DCA (1,2-Dichloroethane)**
- **Prevalence**: 2/5 boreholes (concentrated in charged boreholes)
- **Trend**: DECREASING overall but volatile (-6.45 ppb/year in R-001, R²=0.40)
- **Severity**: High (100 ppb in 1999, persists at 40 ppb in 2008)
- **Source**: Solvent/chemical production byproduct
- **Concern**: Resistance to degradation; depth persistence problematic

**Chloroform**
- **Prevalence**: 2/5 boreholes (R-001, R-004)
- **Trend**: DECREASING but stable (-0.83 ppb/year)
- **Severity**: Moderate-High (20-27 ppb range)
- **Source**: Disinfection byproduct or older spill
- **Concern**: Long-term persistence; possible future remobilization

#### 2. DECAY PRODUCTS
- **cis-1,2-DCE**: Detected (1.04 ppb in R-001, 1999) - confirms TCE biodegradation
- **trans-1,2-DCE**: Detected (2.3 ppb in R-001, 1999) - alternative pathway
- **Significance**: Presence indicates active anaerobic biotransformation

#### 3. HEAVY METALS
- **Expected in zone**: Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu, Pb, Cd (from I-003 metal processing)
- **Status**: Limited data in historical records; recommend expanded analysis

#### 4. PFAS (Emerging)
- **Status**: NOT in TAHAL 2008 data (predates PFAS awareness)
- **Recommendation**: Add PFOA, PFOS to monitoring parameters post-2021

## FORENSIC ANALYSIS

### Source Attribution Framework

**Contamination Pattern 1**: TCE + Chlorinated Compounds Signature
- **Boreholes**: R-001, R-004
- **Confidence**: HIGH
- **Attributed Source**: Chemical Manufacturing Facility A (I-001)
- **Distance**: 250-300m south of boreholes
- **Evidence**:
  - TCE is solvent in organic chemistry
  - 1,2-DCA common in chlorinated compound synthesis
  - Chloroform possible solvent or byproduct
  - Spatial alignment with facility location
  - Temporal alignment with facility operation

**Contamination Pattern 2**: Pharmaceutical Residues
- **Boreholes**: R-002 (potential)
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM
- **Attributed Source**: Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Facility B (I-002)
- **Distance**: 450-500m south-southeast
- **Evidence**:
  - Dichloromethane common in pharmaceutical synthesis
  - Facility 500m downgradient position
  - Limited data availability

### Temporal Forensics

**Phase 1 (1999-2002)**: Initial Contamination Baseline
- High 1,2-DCA (100 ppb in R-001) suggests recent spill or ongoing source
- TCE low (5.2 ppb) suggesting different source or earlier contamination
- Chloroform stable indicating background level

**Phase 2 (2002-2006)**: Rising TCE, Declining 1,2-DCA
- TCE increases sharply (+2.84 ppb/year)
- 1,2-DCA drops from 100 to 50 ppb
- Possible source changes or remediation activities at facility

**Phase 3 (2006-2008)**: Stabilization
- TCE plateaus at 27.7-31.8 ppb
- 1,2-DCA stabilizes at 40 ppb
- Chloroform continues slow decline
- Suggests system reaching new equilibrium

### Plume Migration Model

**East-to-West Flow Path**:
1. Contaminants released from I-001 (south)
2. Travel through permeable A2 layer (~1-5 m/year)
3. Detected in R-001, R-003 (shallow boreholes) 1-5 years after release
4. Reach deeper layers (B2, B3) over 10-20 years
5. Detected in R-004, R-005 (deep boreholes) with time lag
6. Continue west toward Mediterranean discharge zone

**Estimated Travel Times**:
- Shallow aquifer (A2): 2-5 years to reach monitoring network
- Deep aquifer (B3): 15-30 years (confined flow, less direct path)

**Stable Trends** (→):
- TCE in R-004 (deep aquifer) relatively stable
- Indicates deep aquifer has reached equilibrium with contamination source
- May represent aged contamination not rapidly changing

## CONCLUSION

Raanana Industrial Zone shows **MEDIUM-RISK contamination profile** with mixed trend signals. The zone demonstrates improving contamination for 1,2-DCA and chloroform, but concerning upward trend for TCE in the shallow aquifer. Deep aquifer contamination at 88.5m depth indicates historical contamination requiring long-term monitoring.

**Primary Action**: Continue intensive quarterly monitoring, particularly for TCE trend reversal. Investigate facility I-001 operations to determine if 2006-2008 contamination changes reflect source reduction or remediation activities. Validate 2021 Report measurements to confirm trend continuation or reversal.

**Secondary Actions**: Expand parameter suite to include PFAS and decay products; enhance plume mapping; establish contingency water supply alternatives.

**Overall Assessment**: Raanana zone does not require emergency intervention but warrants elevated monitoring priority and proactive management engagement with industrial facilities.
