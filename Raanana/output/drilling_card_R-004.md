## CRITICAL FINDINGS

### 1. Deep Aquifer Contamination
**Issue**: Detecting contaminants at 88.5m depth is highly concerning because:
- Deep layers typically have slower groundwater flow
- Contaminants at depth may not attenuate naturally
- Indicates historical contamination (requires years to reach depth)
- May signal failure of confining layer between zones

### 2. 1,2-DCA Persistence
**Issue**: 1,2-DCA remains elevated (40 ppb) despite 9 years of decline:
- Chemical is resistant to biodegradation
- May accumulate in low-flow zones
- Requires active remediation if natural attenuation inadequate

### 3. TCE Volatility
**Issue**: 2004 drop followed by 2006 recovery suggests:
- Possible contamination plume migration
- Changes in aquifer chemistry affecting solubility
- Potential new releases from source
- Data quality concerns for 2004
